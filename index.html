Melbourne Housing Price For The Past 2016-2017



For the previous few years, Melbourne has been the fourth fastest expanding market in the country, behind Canberra, Sydney, and Hobart. Housing costs in the city range from $500 to $10,000 per square metre. Housing prices are also affected by distance, the closer a unit is to the CBD, the higher the price. Suburbs such as Port Melboune, Southbank, Collingwood, and Carlton has a land price of $5,000 per square metre. Meanwhile, suburbs such as Gisbone, Emerald, Epping has a relatively lower land price.



How Does Age of House Affecting The Price?

The majority of the houses that are available in the market was built after the 1900s. It appears that the size of the land has little to no bearing on the price, while small house built later have a greater price than older houses with larger land size built earlier.

Townhouse and Units has a range of $3,000 or lower which is significantly lower compared to House's price range that could be up to $9,000. Most of the townhouse and units were built in after the 1950s with a smaller size compared to the ones that were built early 1900s.
Majority of houses have 3 to 4 rooms in it with a price range bellow $5,000.

How Does Price Evolve Overtime?

In this graph, there is a recurring pattern in which the number of units sold drops below 20 units for all regions in the beginning of December. This is followed by a massive surge in every location over the next few days. During the period, the Northern Metropolitan Area had the highest number of units sold. While the number of units sold in parts of Victoria has the lowest total units sold during the period span.



Which Method Used The Most?

The most common method of selling the property is by a stated price (Property Sold) with a total of 4373. Meanwhile, there are only a few units that were sold after an auction. The number of units sold after an auction in each month was below 10 units.

Top 15 Agencies In 2016-2017

In a two-year span, Nelson Alexander agents sold 817 homes, followed by Jellis Craig with 701 homes sold. McGrath and Sweeney, on the other hand, have the lowest number of sales, with 105 and 106 respectively.
