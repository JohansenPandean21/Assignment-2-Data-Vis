Melbourne Housing Price For The Past 2016-2017



For the previous few years, Melbourne has been the fourth fastest expanding market in the country, behind Canberra, Sydney, and Hobart. Housing costs in the city range from $500 to $10,000 per square metre. Housing prices are also affected by distance, the closer a unit is to the CBD, the higher the price. Suburbs such as Port Melboune, Southbank, Collingwood, and Carlton has a land price of $5,000 per square metre. Meanwhile, suburbs such as Gisbone, Emerald, Epping has a relatively lower land price. How Does Age of House Affecting The Price?

The majority of the houses that are available in the market was built after the 1900s. It appears that the size of the land has little to no bearing on the price, while small house built later have a greater price than older houses with larger land size built earlier.

Townhouse and Units has a range of $3,000 or lower which is significantly lower compared to House's price range that could be up to $9,000. Most of the townhouse and units were built in after the 1950s with a smaller size compared to the ones that were built early 1900s.
